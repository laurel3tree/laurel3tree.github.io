Music

I started taking guitar lessons at Moze Guitars in La Mesa in 2017 with the goal of learning to play the blues. It was there I met classical guitarist Brandon Cruz and, together, we discovered a passion for bossa nova. The first song we recorded was a Brazilian standard called "Carinhoso", written in 1917 by Pixinguinha (Alfredo Vianna) with lyrics by João de Barro.

I love the low-key nature of bossa nova. It's a deceptively simple genre; underneath the relaxed lounge vibe are complex rhythms and exotic harmonies. Whereas most pop songs consist of 3-5 chords, bossa nova can have upwards of 30 chords (not including chord variations) in a single piece. Take for example "Corcovado", with its lilting melody and soft accompaniment. This song has 17 unique chords!

One of my favorite Brazilian duos of all-time is Nara Leão and Roberto Menescal. I dig absolutely everything about this vintage performance of "O barquinho", "O pato", and "Manhã de carnaval"—down to Nara's chic haircut and white overalls. I secretly hope Brandon and I get reincarnated as this couple.
